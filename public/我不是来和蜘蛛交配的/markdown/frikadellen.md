> German **Frikadellen** (also called *Buletten* or *Fleischpflanzerl* depending on the region) are juicy, well-seasoned beef and pork patties. They're thicker and softer than burgers, with a distinctive onion and mustard flavour.

### Ingredients (Makes 8)

* 500g beef mince (15–20% fat)
* 500g pork mince
* 1 large onion, very finely diced
* 2 tbsp butter
* 2 stale bread rolls (or 120g white bread), soaked in milk then squeezed dry
* 2 eggs
* 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
* 2 tsp salt
* 1 tsp black pepper
* 1 tsp sweet paprika
* ½ tsp marjoram (optional but traditional)
* 2 tbsp chopped parsley
* Neutral oil or lard for frying

### Method

1. Gently cook the onion in the butter for 8–10 minutes until soft and sweet. Cool completely.
2. Tear the soaked bread into small pieces.
3. Combine everything in a large bowl.
4. Mix thoroughly by hand for 3–5 minutes until the mixture becomes sticky and cohesive. This develops the proteins and gives the proper texture.
5. Chill for at least 30 minutes.
6. Shape into 8 patties about 2cm thick.
7. Heat a large frying pan over medium heat with a little oil or lard.
8. Fry for **5–6 minutes per side**, lowering the heat if they brown too quickly. The internal temperature should reach **70°C**.

### To serve

Traditionally with:

* Hot German potato salad
* Potato mash
* Sauerkraut
* Mustard
* Gherkins

They're also excellent cold the next day in a crusty roll with mustard and sliced onion.

### Tips

* A 50:50 mix of beef and pork is much juicier than beef alone.
* The soaked bread is essential—it keeps the Frikadellen tender rather than dense.
* Don't press them down while frying; let a good crust develop naturally.
* Rest for 5 minutes before serving to keep them juicy.
